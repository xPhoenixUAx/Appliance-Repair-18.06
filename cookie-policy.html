2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be session cookies that expire when you close your browser, persistent cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them, first-party cookies set by the website you are visiting, or third-party cookies set by a domain other than the one you are visiting.

5. How to Manage Cookies

You have the right to accept or reject cookies. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify your browser settings to decline cookies if you prefer.

You can control cookies through browser settings in Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Safari, Microsoft Edge, and other browsers. You may also use third-party opt-out tools such as Google Analytics Opt-Out, the Digital Advertising Alliance, and the Network Advertising Initiative.

Blocking or deleting cookies may impact your experience on our website. Some features may not function properly if cookies are disabled.
